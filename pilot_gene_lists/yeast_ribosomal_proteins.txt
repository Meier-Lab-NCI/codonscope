# Yeast (S. cerevisiae) cytoplasmic ribosomal protein genes (~128 genes)
# Source: SGD GO:0003735 (structural constituent of ribosome)
# References:
#   Ikemura T, 'Codon usage and tRNA content in unicellular and multicellular
#     organisms', Mol Biol Evol 2:13-34 (1985).
#   Sharp PM & Li WH, 'The codon adaptation index — a measure of directional
#     synonymous codon usage bias, and its potential applications', Nucleic Acids
#     Res 15:1281-1295 (1987).
# Yeast RP genes are the canonical example of translational selection on synonymous
# codons. Both paralogs (A and B) are included where they exist.
# Expected CodonScope results:
#   Mode 1: Extreme codon optimization — AAG over AAA (Lys), GGT over other Gly,
#     TTG over other Leu, GCT over other Ala, AGA over other Arg
#   Mode 2: Moderate Z-scores (~1.7) because RP genes dominate ~72% of yeast
#     translational demand — they ARE the background
#   Mode 3: Highest tAI/wtAI scores in the genome; visible 5' ramp (first 30-50
#     codons have lower optimality than the gene body)
#   Mode 4: Low FS enrichment ratio (~1.0 or below); high FF proportion —
#     highly expressed genes minimize ribosome collisions
#   Mode 5: Synonymous-driven attribution — amino acid composition is normal,
#     but synonymous codon choice is strongly skewed toward tRNA-abundant codons.
#     Drivers: tRNA supply and wobble avoidance.
#   Mode 6: Low RSCU correlation with human orthologs (mean r ~0.13) — yeast and
#     human use different preferred codons for the same amino acids
