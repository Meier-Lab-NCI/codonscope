# Human 5'TOP mRNAs — mTOR-regulated translational targets (115 genes)
# Source: Compiled from multiple studies characterizing 5' terminal
#   oligopyrimidine (TOP) motif-containing mRNAs:
#   Thoreen CC et al., 'A unifying model for mTORC1-mediated regulation of
#     mRNA translation', Nature 485:109-113 (2012).
#   Philippe L et al., 'Global analysis of LARP1 translation targets reveals
#     tunable and dynamic features of 5' TOP motifs', Proc Natl Acad Sci USA
#     117:5319-5328 (2020).
#   Cockman E et al., 'TOP mRNPs: molecular mechanisms and principles of
#     regulation', Biomolecules 10:969 (2020). [Review]
#   Meyuhas O & Kahan T, 'The race to decipher the top secrets of TOP mRNAs',
#     Biochim Biophys Acta 1849:801-811 (2015).
#   Iadevaia V et al., 'All translation elongation factors and the e, f, and h
#     subunits of translation initiation factor 3 are encoded by 5'-terminal
#     oligopyrimidine (TOP) mRNAs', RNA 14:1730-1736 (2008).
# The 5'TOP motif consists of a 5'-cytidine followed by 4-15 pyrimidines.
# These mRNAs are translationally repressed when mTOR is inhibited (via
# LARP1 binding) and activated when mTOR is active (growth signals).

#     ribosomal proteins) and synonymous-driven components
#   Key feature: this gene set is TRANSLATIONALLY regulated — the codon
#     bias reflects evolutionary optimization for high translational output,
#     not transcriptional regulation
